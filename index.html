Kindred Series

Kindred Flow maps family history through time.

Kindred Flow is a genealogy viewer produced by Parthenian, LLC as part of the Kindred series of products. It turns family-tree data into an interactive map and timeline so you can see where people lived, how families moved, and how branches relate across places and generations.

You can load GEDCOM family-tree files, select one or more trees, animate migration over time, inspect people and clusters, explore the selected data with evidence-backed questions, and share trees with other signed-in users.

Sign in on any device to see the trees you own and the trees shared with you. You can load GEDCOM family-tree files, save loaded tree data on Kindred servers for 7 days free of charge, and share tree data with other signed-in users. Shared tree data is stored on Kindred servers, and you may delete or update it at any time.

Before you upload a tree

Kindred Flow loads your family tree into browser memory so you can explore it on the map and timeline.

If you are signed in and save the tree, Kindred stores a server copy for 7 days free of charge. Saved trees are associated with your account and can be updated by uploading a tree with the same name.

On desktop, the Trees panel lets you name saved trees and share them with specific people by email. Shared data is stored on Kindred servers while it is available to those people.
